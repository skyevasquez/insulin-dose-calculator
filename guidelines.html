Insulin Types & Timing

L

Lantus/Semglee

Long-acting Basal Insulin

Daily Dose 15 units

Instructions:

  • Take 1 time per day at the same time everyday
  • Do not take when sick or not eating

Duration: 24 hours
Onset: 1-2 hours
Peak: Minimal peak

N

Novolog

Rapid-acting Meal Insulin

Purpose:

  • Insulin for meals (meal dose)
  • Correct high blood sugar (correction dose)

Instructions:

  • Take 10 minutes before eating & lasts 4 hours
  • Check sugar before each meal
  • Total Dose = Meal Dose + Correction Dose
  • Do not take if last dose was less than 4 hours ago

Duration: 4 hours
Onset: 10-15 minutes
Peak: 1-2 hours

Safety Protocols

!

Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)

Symptoms:

  • • Shakiness, sweating
  • • Fast heartbeat
  • • Hunger, irritability
  • • Dizziness, confusion

Treatment (15-15 Rule):

  1. 1. Take 15g fast-acting carbs
  2. 2. Wait 15 minutes
  3. 3. Recheck blood sugar
  4. 4. Repeat if still low
⚕

Sick Day Guidelines

When Sick:

  • • Check blood sugar more frequently
  • • Continue taking Lantus (unless vomiting)
  • • Stay hydrated
  • • Contact healthcare provider if concerned

Call Healthcare Provider If:

  • • Blood sugar >300 mg/dL
  • • Vomiting for more than 4 hours
  • • Signs of dehydration
  • • Ketones in urine

Best Practices

💉

Injection Technique

  • Rotate injection sites
  • Insert needle at 90° angle
  • Hold for 5-10 seconds
  • Don't rub injection site
🌡️

Storage

  • Refrigerate unopened vials
  • Room temperature for current use
  • Avoid extreme temperatures
  • Check expiration dates
📊

Monitoring

  • Check blood sugar regularly
  • Keep a log
  • Watch for patterns
  • Share with healthcare team
